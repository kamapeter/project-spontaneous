What is a Force?

Force is basically a push or a pull. 300 years ago after making thorough observations, Isaac Newton put forth his conclusions in the laws of motion. From the first law of motion we can derive a comprehensive definition of force.

The law states:

"Every body will continue in it's present state of rest or continue moving in a straight line with uniform speed unless a force acts on it".

This means that: When stationary bodies begin to move a force must be acting on them and when moving bodies stop moving or change their speed,a force is also acting on them. The following animations illustrate this:

Module2

INERTIA

Inertia refers to the tendency of a body to remain in its state of rest or uniform motion.

In other words inertia is responsible for stationary bodies remaining stationary and moving bodies remaining in motion. Since Newton first law is similar to the above statement, it is also known as the law of inertia.

Bigger bodies have bigger inertia in other words as the mass of a body increases its inertia also increases, hence more force is required to move a truck (big body) than a bicycle(small body) because the truck has more inertia than the bicycle. Conversely more force is required to stop a moving truck than a moving bike

Physics In Real Life

Inertia

We experience the effect of inertia in everyday life. For example when a fast moving car suddenly comes to rest,its passengers jerk forward. Why? The concept of inertia explains this: when the car was in motion its passengers where also moving they possess inertia and would therefore tend to continue to move hence they jerked forward. This can lead to serious accidents hence seat belts are provided to reduce the effect of inertia.

Naturally once bodies begin to move they should continue moving infinitely but this is not always the case because other forces such as gravity, friction and air resistance acts to resist their motion thereby changing it (stopping or reducing) just as predicted by Newtons first law.

Momentum

In order to fully understand forces we must consider a second quantity called " Momentum".

Momentum is defined mathematically as the product of mass and velocity. That is mass×velocity.

It is usually denoted by P. therefore:

P = m × v

The unit of Momentum is Kgms-1 or Ns. We would shortly explain the last unit.

Hence if a body has a mass of 10kg and a velocity of ms-1, its momentum is:
P = m × v
P=10kg×5ms -1
P=50Kgms -1 or 50Ns

Physics In Real Life

Momentum

The direct consequence of the definition of momentum is that,Bigger bodies have more momentum than smaller bodies when they move with they move with the same velocity.

This explains why a large truck has more destructive effect on objects it hits than a small car even when they move with same speed.

Module 4

Newton's Second Law

Newton's second law helps us to quantitatively define force.

It states that: "The rate of change of momentum of a body is directly proportional to the applied force and takes place in its direction".

What this means: When there is a greater change in the momentum of a body, a great force is acting on it. But when the change in momentum is small,it is because a small force is acting on it.

Net Forces

Net Force

In most cases more than one force acts on a body. In that case, the total effect of those forces on a body is known as the Net Force. Forces are vector quantities thus they have both direction and magnitude. So the effect of the forces acting on a body depend on their respective magnitude and direction. The net force has the same effect as the original Forces acting on it. For example if two boys pull a toy in opposite direction with a force of 5N each. The resultant force is :
F = 5N +(-5N) = 0.
Or we say no net force acts on the toy. 0N can replace the forces acting on it. This explains why the body does not accelerate.

If on the on the other hand the two boys push the toy with a force of 5N each facing the same direction, the net Force is
F = 5N + 5N = 10N
10N can replace the two forces acting on the toy and produce the same effect. This explains why a greater acceleration is produced.

Hence Newton's Second Law can be correctly restated as :

If a net external force act on a body it Accelerates in the direction of the Net Force.

Newton's Third Law of Motion

Newton third law of motion helps us to understand a fundamental characteristic of force.

It States that: For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

What this means: when a force acts on a body (this is an action) the body exerts another force. (this is the reaction) The magnitude of the reaction is equal to the action but its direction is opposite that of the action

Physics In Real Life

Newton's Third Law

We experience the effects of newton's third law everyday for example:
