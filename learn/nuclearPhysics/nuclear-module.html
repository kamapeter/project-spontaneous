Concept Of Atom

If you take an object, say a yam and keep dividing it with a knife,the particles of the yam would logically become smaller with each division. Ultimately a point would be reached where further division would be impossible. Yes we would have reached the Smallest Constituent of the object (yam). This is what we call an Atom.

Basically an Atom is : A basic building block of matter

But what does an atom "look like"? how does it behave? what is it composition? You'll learn about this in subsequent modules. This module was just to remind you of what you've learnt before

Module 2

Models

In subsequent modules we'll be talking majorly on models so it'll be Nice if you grok what the really are.

A model is A simplified representation used to explain the working of some real world object or system

So basically a model need not be exactly like the object it explains it just needs to explain the real world object consistently. An example of a model would be using stones to teach a child how to calculate sums of money. The stones are not exactly money but can be used to represent money thus making it easy for the child to sum up amounts of money. The child can easily add up stones and count. Hence the stones are models of a real world object—money.

In physics, we can't always comprehend all the workings nature hence we use models— things we can easily visualize to explain them. the models need not have a true resemblance of the real world object, They just need to explain them consistently

Module 3

Thompson's Model of the Atom

Basis:In 1897 J.J Thomson based on his experiments discovered that atoms contain negatively charged particles called electrons.

He also measured their mass to charge ratio which indicated they were very tiny.

In a bid to explain his findings, Thomson proposed a model of the atom known as the "Plum Pudding Model".

He proposed that the atom is:

  • A sphere of positive charge with negative charges (electrons) surrounding it.
  • The magnitude of the positive charge is equal to the magnitude of the negative charges hence the atom is electrically neutral (positive and negative neutralize).

    • Thus Thomson's model explains why the atom could contain negative charge and still remain electrically neutral.

    Limitations

    Rutherford's Model

    Rutherford's model of the atom is not without it's limitatotions.
    According to a proven electromagnetic theory, any accelerating charge emits radiation and hence it's energy reduces.
    According to thomson's model electrons revolve around the nucleus and hence emits radiation. The kind of radiation observed was of the line spectrum (single momentary emmissions) but that predicted by thomson's model was of the continous spectrum (series of emissions) because the electrons were continually moving and the frequency of radiation depends on the frequency of revolution of the electrons.
    Another consequence of thomson's model was that the electrons would continue to loose energy (since the emit it) and would ultimately collapse into the nucleus. So why should an atom exist? clearly a new model was needed...

    Module 5

    Bohr's model of the Atom

    Basis: Niels bohr proposed a model of the atom in 1913 inorder to explain the inconsistencies in thomson's model.
    He held that:
  • Electrons move round the nucleus in certain stable orbits without emitting radiation.
  • A definite amount of energy is attributed to each stable orbit.
  • Electrons emit or absorb radiation in discrete packets when jumping from one orbit to another.

    • Thus bohr's model could solve the stability problem (electrons collapsing into the atom) since it held that electrons do not emit radiation (are stable) when moving round the nucleus.
    The next slide explains this more...→

    Under The Lens

    Bohr's model of the Atom

    Instead of having one orbit, bohr's model suggest many orbits denoted by n.
  • Each orbit has a specific energy requirement (energy levels)
  • Energy levels are discrete that is can only be integers,n=1,2,3... and hence there are no in-between states like 1.5,2.5...
  • Just like the rungs of a ladder
  • In moving from a lower energy level say n=1,to a higher level say n=2, energy is absorbed by the lectron and is said to be excited
  • This energy is absorbed in form of a photon (packet of e-m radiation)
  • This energy is dependent on the the frequency of the photon and is given by

    E=En-Ep = hf
    Ee= Ep+ hf


    E is energy absorbed (energy of the photon),En is the energy of a higher orbit,Ep is energy of the present orbit,h = 6.626×10-34Js known as the planck constant and f is frequency of absorbed photon.
  • In moving from a higher to a lower level energy is emitted in form of a photon.
  • The orbit closest to the nucleus is known as the ground state and requires no absoption of energy. It is the most stable.

    Atomic Structure

    The atom is basically made up of the nucleus and electrons That orbit it.
    Electrons are negatively charged and hace a very small mass.
    When looking deep in to the nucleus we find out that the nucleus is basically made upof protons and neutrons.
    Protons are positively charged particles with a mass greater than that of electrons.
    Neutrons are chargeless (neutral) and are heavier than electrons.
    The number of protons and electrons in any neutral atom are equal in order to ensure neutrality.

    Atomic Structure Notatation.

    The composition of any atom can be conviniently represented using the following notation AZX. A represents mass number. Z represents the atomic number.
    Atomic number is the number of protons an atom contains.
    Mass number is the sum of protons and neutrons in an atom.Thus:

    A = Z + N

    Where N is the number of neutrons. Thus the lithium atom drawn in the previous slide can be represented as 53Li. The mass number lithium (sum of protons and neutrons) is 5, the atomic number (proton) is 3. Number of neutrons:
    N = A -Z = 5-3=2.
